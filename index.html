WHAT IS LEGO ART?

Can you make art from Lego? There seems to be widespread media agreement that you can. Nathan Sawaya has an exhibition of ambitious Lego models at Old Truman Brewery in London. Reports uncritically call it “art”, made by an “artist”. Is that so?

Certainly these Lego statues aspire to the kinds of existential anguish you might see in serious art. There’s a figure pulling open its own chest – to reveal bricks pouring out from within – and another holding up a Lego mask as if meditating on the enigma of identity.

Serious stuff! In fact, some of the sculptor Antony Gormley’s work also looks like Lego – his giant figure squatting on a London hotel resembles a superscale Lego man. So maybe Lego modelling and contemporary art really are coming together. This mix is called Lego Art.
